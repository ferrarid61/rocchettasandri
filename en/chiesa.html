Church of San Giovanni Battista

A jewel of Apennine Renaissance architecture — from 1553

The Facade and the Renaissance Portal

The present Parish Church of Rocchetta Sandri, dedicated to San Giovanni Battista (Saint John the Baptist) — patron saint of the village, celebrated on 24 June — dates back to 1553, as attested by the date engraved on the side portal.

The facade is essential, with a wall framed by two slightly outlined corner pilasters, on which only the portal and the rose window stand out. The portal is decorated with Renaissance friezes, an element of refined craftsmanship that pleasantly contrasts with the sobriety of the whole.

"The simplicity of the facade does not prepare the visitor for the surprise of the interior, where the fifteenth-century spatial conception reveals itself in all its elegance."

The Interior: the Barrel Vault and the Chapels

The interior surprises with its spatial conception, in a fifteenth-century idiom. The plan consists of an elongated hall covered by a barrel vault punctuated by five arches, which gives the space a solemn and measured rhythm.

Opening off the hall are two side chapels set beneath the lunettes, which once housed two valuable paintings depicting Saint Stephen and Saint Anthony. Of notable interest is also the 18th-century piece of furniture in the sacristy, a testament to the refined quality of local craftsmanship.

The Wooden Baptistry and the Sculptural Elements

The left-hand altar is preceded, towards the entrance, by a smaller niche which houses one of the most precious pieces of the entire church: a wooden baptistry in the form of a small central-plan temple, clearly Bramante-inspired in conception.

This carved wooden work, with its central plan and harmonious proportions, represents a rare example of architectural sculpture applied to sacred furnishings in the Modenese Apennines.

🏛️

Portal with Renaissance friezes

The side portal, dated 1553, is decorated with Renaissance friezes carved in local stone — the only ornamental element on an otherwise sober facade.

⛪

Barrel vault with five bays

The internal barrel-vaulted ceiling, punctuated by five transverse arches, recalls the architectural models of the Tuscan-Emilian Quattrocento.

🪵

Bramante-inspired wooden baptistry

A small central-plan temple carved in wood, in the style of Bramante: a unique piece in the Frignano area for its craftsmanship and spatial conception.

🪣

Sandstone stoup

The holy-water stoup by the side portal, carved in sandstone in the typical basin shape set into the wall, may date from the earlier church, before its destruction in 1535.

A Link with the Earlier Church

Some elements of the present church may have even older origins. The holy-water stoup by the side portal — carved in sandstone in the typical basin shape set into the wall — may be a reuse from the primitive church, the one destroyed in 1535 on the orders of the ducal commissioner Zanelli as retaliation for the inhabitants' participation in the Sack of Sestola.

The present church was thus built a few decades after that destruction, in 1553, as a spiritual and material rebirth of the community of Rocchetta.
